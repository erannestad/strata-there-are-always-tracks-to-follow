There Are Always Tracks To Follow

About the Project

There Are Always Tracks to Follow references a 2016 archeologic excavation of the Harvard Indian College. This abstract container posits that archeology is an act of making and unmaking mythology of place, tracing systemic forces through the stratigraphy of colonialism from the shal- lowest layers to its depths.

Objects like bricks, bones, old nails, and lead block type from the Eliot Bible mark a history where the 17th-century settler-colonial project intersected with indigenous Massachusett, Wampanoag, and Nipmuck peoples through complex interactions of trade, violence, manipulation, and territorial dispossession.

Excavating these historical layers meant sifting through contemporary deposits: shredded plastic, petrochemical sediments, modern currency, and traces of a campus transformed by historical forces.

These contemporary artifacts were returned to the ground or discarded, leaving unexamined the myths embedded in these material remnants.

There Are Always Tracks to Follow is an art installation functioning as an excavation site, framing artifacts as an archive of interconnected layers revealing massively distributed forces acting across time. By transforming archaeological findings into an immersive experience, the artwork challenges traditional historical interpretation.

The installation integrates a web-based augmented reality (webAR) experience of a swirling mass of archived objects from the dig site and contemporary artifacts. The artwork challenges traditional methods of historical interpretation and invites viewers to engage with layers of historical and contemporary artifacts.
